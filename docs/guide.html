Jane Chart Assistant

Quick Start Guide for Bulk Chart Export
By Brightlight Health

What this tool does

Jane Chart Assistant is a Chrome extension that exports patient chart PDFs from Jane App in bulk — instead of clicking through every patient and every chart by hand, it runs up to 8 browser tabs in parallel and saves everything into organized folders on your computer.

Everything happens locally in your browser. The extension only talks to Jane App's own servers; nothing is sent anywhere else.

Good for: clinic transitions, compliance archives, end-of-year backups, data migration projects.
Before you start: the browser tabs must stay open for the duration of the run. Large clinics (10,000+ patients) should budget an overnight window. Disable sleep/lock on your computer so it doesn't pause mid-run.

What you need

  • Google Chrome (or any Chromium browser — Edge, Brave, Arc). Safari and Firefox are not supported.
  • A Jane App login with permission to view the charts you want to export.
  • The jane-chart-assistant.zip file you received (bundled with this guide).

Install the extension

One-time setup, about two minutes.

1

Unzip the file you received.

Save the unzipped folder somewhere you won't move or delete it — for example, Documents/jane-chart-assistant. If you ever delete that folder, the extension will stop working.

2

Open Chrome's extensions page.

Type this into the Chrome address bar and press Enter:

chrome://extensions
3

Turn on Developer mode.

Find the Developer mode switch in the top-right corner of the extensions page and flip it on. Three new buttons — Load unpacked, Pack extension, Update — will appear at the top-left.

Developer mode toggle (top right) flipped on; Load unpacked appears at top left.

4

Click "Load unpacked" and pick the unzipped folder.

In the file picker, navigate to the folder you unzipped in step 1 — the folder itself, not a file inside it. It's the one that contains manifest.json.

The folder to select contains manifest.json, the src/ subfolder, and the icon PNGs.

After you click Select, the extension installs. A new tile labeled Jane Chart Assistant appears on the extensions page:

Confirm the tile is present and its toggle is on (blue).

5

Pin the extension to the toolbar.

Click the puzzle-piece icon in Chrome's toolbar. In the menu that opens, find "Jane Chart Assistant" and click the pin icon next to it. The Brightlight logo now sits in your toolbar for one-click access.

Click the pin icon next to Jane Chart Assistant to add it to your toolbar.

About the yellow banner. Chrome will show "Disable developer mode extensions" every time it starts. Click × to dismiss — do not click Disable, or the extension will stop working until you re-enable it.

Your first export — walkthrough

Example scenario

Dr. Alex runs Example Clinic, whose Jane URL is https://exampleclinic.janeapp.com. They want to archive every patient chart from their clinic onto their laptop. Alex signs in to Jane App as alex@example.com.

1

Click the Brightlight logo in your Chrome toolbar.

A side panel opens on the right side of the window showing the entry form and two mode buttons — Export Charts (for bulk chart PDF export) and Migration (for appointments + patients export).

The side panel at startup — stats at top, the three shared form fields (clinic, email, password), and the two mode buttons.

2

Click "Export Charts".

A form appears with fields for clinic name, email, password, number of threads, and an optional Max Patient ID.

3

Fill in the form.

FieldWhat Alex entersNotes
Clinic NameexampleclinicThe subdomain from the Jane URL — everything before .janeapp.com.
Emailalex@example.comThe email used to sign in to Jane App.
Password(Alex's password)Stored locally only while the run is active.
Number of Threads44 is a safe default. 1 is the slowest and safest. 8 is fastest but more likely to hit rate limits.
Max Patient ID(blank)Leave blank to process every patient. Fill in to stop after a specific ID.
4

Click Start.

Chrome will show a permission dialog — this is expected and only happens the first time you export from a given clinic:

Allow "Jane Chart Assistant" to:
• Read and change your data on exampleclinic.janeapp.com
Block Allow

Click Allow. This grants access to only Alex's clinic subdomain — not to any other Jane clinic, and not to any other website. Chrome remembers the grant, so the dialog won't appear again for this clinic.

5

Watch the export run.

The extension opens one browser tab per thread (4 tabs in this example), logs in to each, and starts walking through patient records. The side panel switches to a running view:

It's normal to see a few warnings — the extension handles rate limits and transient failures automatically. The tabs must stay open, but you can minimize Chrome or switch to other windows.

6

When it finishes, find the files in your Downloads folder.

Every chart lands in ~/Downloads/jane-scraper/, organized one folder per patient:

~/Downloads/
  └── jane-scraper/
      ├── 127_Sarah_Johnson/
      │   ├── Consultation_Note__4521.pdf
      │   ├── Followup__4810.pdf
      │   └── Lab_Results__5012.pdf
      ├── 128_Michael_Chen/
      │   ├── Initial_Assessment__4602.pdf
      │   └── Treatment_Plan__4785.pdf
      └── ...
Tip: resume mid-run. If you interrupt an export and restart it, the extension detects already-downloaded files by filename and skips them — so you can safely stop/start without re-downloading everything.

Stopping an export

Click the red Stop Export button in the side panel. The extension finishes the chart it's currently on, closes the worker tabs, and stops adding to Downloads. Any files already saved stay put.

Troubleshooting

SymptomWhat to do
"Disable developer mode extensions" bannerClick × to dismiss. Don't click Disable.
Side panel doesn't openGo to chrome://extensions, toggle the extension off and back on.
Permission dialog didn't appearCheck the clinic name field — no https://, no .janeapp.com, just the subdomain (e.g. exampleclinic).
"Login failed" in the logTry logging in manually at https://<clinic>.janeapp.com first to confirm the credentials work.
Frequent "rate limit detected" messagesNormal in moderation. If constant, reduce thread count to 2 or 1 and restart.
Laptop goes to sleep mid-runmacOS: System Settings → Lock Screen → set display-off and screen-saver to Never. Windows: Settings → Power → Screen and sleep → Never.
All tabs go idleClick Stop Export, wait 10s, then click Export Charts again. Already-saved files will be skipped on the resume.
Need to remove the extensionchrome://extensions → Jane Chart Assistant → Remove.

Privacy

  • The extension does not send patient data, credentials, or usage data anywhere other than directly between your browser and Jane App's own servers.
  • All exported PDFs are stored only on your local computer.
  • Credentials are held in Chrome's local storage only while an export is running and are never transmitted elsewhere.
  • The full privacy policy is included in the extension folder (PRIVACY_POLICY.md).
